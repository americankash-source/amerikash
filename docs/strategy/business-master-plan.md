# AmeriKash Business Master Plan

## Business Vision
Build AmeriKash into a trusted AI financial planning platform with multiple revenue paths: consumer subscription, advisor tooling, and embedded planning APIs.

## Core Business Thesis
Financial advice is expensive, fragmented, and often inaccessible. AmeriKash can create value by delivering explainable, actionable, AI-assisted planning at a lower cost and higher convenience.

## Revenue Models

### 1. Consumer SaaS
Offer free basic planning and paid premium planning.

Potential pricing:
- Free: basic cashflow/risk snapshot
- Plus: $15-$30/month for saved plans, scenarios, and insights
- Premium: higher tier for advanced planning and coaching workflows

### 2. Advisor/Planner Tools
Provide financial professionals with client intake, plan generation, and scenario modeling.

Potential pricing:
- $99-$499/month per advisor or team

### 3. Embedded API
Expose planning intelligence to fintech apps, banks, credit unions, and payroll/benefits platforms.

Potential pricing:
- Usage-based API pricing
- Platform fee + per-plan fee
- Enterprise contracts

### 4. Affiliate/Referral Revenue
Potential later revenue from carefully disclosed referrals to financial products.

Examples:
- Brokerage accounts
- Savings accounts
- Credit products
- Insurance products

This must be handled with clear disclosures and compliance review.

## Initial Go-To-Market Strategy

### Beachhead
Young professionals and emerging earners who want guidance but cannot justify a financial advisor.

### Initial Channels
- Personal finance content
- SEO calculators and planning tools
- Beta waitlist
- Creator partnerships
- Financial literacy communities
- Advisor discovery calls

## Positioning
AmeriKash should not position as just another budgeting app.

Recommended positioning:

> AmeriKash is an AI financial planning engine that turns your income, expenses, goals, and risk profile into clear, explainable next steps.

## Differentiators
- Explainable recommendations
- Scenario modeling
- Persistent plan history
- Multi-agent planning engine
- Auditability/trust layer
- Future embedded API potential

## Validation Plan
Before overbuilding, validate:
1. Do users understand the output?
2. Do users trust the guidance?
3. Do users return to update plans?
4. Would users pay for scenario modeling?
5. Would advisors use it for client intake/planning?

## Key Business KPIs
- Waitlist signups
- Cost per signup
- First plan completion rate
- Free-to-paid conversion
- Monthly active users
- Retention after 30/60/90 days
- Scenario usage per user
- Advisor demo requests

## Launch Path

### Phase 1: Private Beta
- Invite small group of users.
- Collect qualitative feedback.
- Validate plan clarity and trust.

### Phase 2: Public Waitlist
- Launch landing page.
- Capture emails.
- Test messaging and pricing hypotheses.

### Phase 3: Paid Pilot
- Offer premium planning features.
- Test willingness to pay.

### Phase 4: B2B Exploration
- Interview advisors and financial coaches.
- Test white-label/client-intake workflows.

## Strategic Recommendation
Build consumer MVP first, but keep backend architecture API-ready so the same engine can later support B2B and embedded finance.
